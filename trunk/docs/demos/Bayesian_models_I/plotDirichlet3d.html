Plot a Dirichlet distribution

Contents

  • alpha = [10 10 10]
  • alpha = [0.1 0.1 0.1]

alpha = [10 10 10]

xyrange = [0 1 0 1];
model.alpha = 10;
npoints = 200;
figure();
plotSurface(@(X)exp(dirichletLogprob(model, X)), xyrange, 'npoints', npoints);
shading interp;
camlight right;
lighting phong;
set(gca, 'xtick', 0:0.5:1, 'ytick', 0:0.5:1);
zlabel('p');
title('alpha = [10 10 10]');
printPmtkFigure alpha10;

alpha = [0.1 0.1 0.1]

Here we plot the log probability - plots of exp(p) when alpha < 1 are ugly due to numerical error, but for alpha = 0.1, the plots of p and logp look almost identical.

model.alpha = 0.1;
npoints = 500; % We need a higher resolution when alpha < 1
figure();
plotSurface(@(X)dirichletLogprob(model, X), xyrange, 'npoints', npoints);
view([-32 50]);
shading interp;
camlight right;
lighting phong;
set(gca, 'xtick', 0:0.5:1, 'ytick', 0:0.5:1);
zlabel('log(p)');
title('alpha = [0.1 0.1 0.1]');
printPmtkFigure alpha0-1;
